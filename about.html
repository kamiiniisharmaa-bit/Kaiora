A sanctuary for the curious & the slow-living

Kaiōra was born from a quiet rebellion against the disposable. We believe objects should age with grace, carry memories, and connect us to the hands that shaped them. Our name — inspired by the Greek "kairos" (the opportune moment) and the Māori "ora" (life, well-being) — celebrates the deep beauty of intentional living.

We travel remote villages, visit forest workshops, and collaborate with ceramicists, weavers, and woodworkers who honor ancestral techniques. Kaiōra is not just a shop; it's a gathering place for stories, textures, and timeless rituals.

Our ethos: soft power, slow craft

We champion transparency, fair wages, and materials that respect the earth. Every object we offer carries the spirit of its maker — and a promise to outlast seasons.

Heritage hands

We work directly with artisans, preserving disappearing crafts and celebrating imperfections as identity.

Planet positive

Carbon-neutral shipping, recycled packaging, and 1% for the Earth — always.

Radical transparency

From kiln to doorstep, we trace every supply chain. No greenwashing, just honesty.

Slow rituals

We encourage one thoughtful purchase over ten rushed ones. Buy well, treasure forever.

The Kaiōra journey

2021

Founders Elena & Kenji leave city life, travel through Japan and Portugal — discovering forgotten glazing techniques and organic linen weavers. The seed of Kaiōra is planted.

2022

Launch of first online collection with 12 ceramicists. Within 6 months, Kaiōra donates $10k to artisan schools in Oaxaca & Morocco.

2024

Opened the Kaiōra Atelier in Portland — a space for workshops, exhibitions, and community gatherings. Featured in "Slow Homes" magazine.

2026

B Corp pending — we’re committed to the highest standards of social and environmental impact. 45+ global artisan partners and growing.

Behind the atelier

“I started Kaiōra after years of collecting vintage pottery and realizing that true value lies in story, not speed. Every glaze drip, every chisel mark is a conversation between maker and material. We hand-select each piece, visit every partner workshop, and ensure that our team receives fair living wages.

Kaiōra is my love letter to the artisans keeping traditions alive — and to the curious souls who seek more than just decoration.”

— Elena Hartwell, Founder & Creative Director

Planet & people first

We offset 150% of our carbon emissions, use 100% recycled or compostable packaging, and donate 2% of every sale to cultural preservation funds. Our pledge: net-zero by 2027.
